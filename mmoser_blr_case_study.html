Case Study · Workplace Strategy

Workplaces that work for everyone

A research-led, data-validated workplace transformation that achieved triple certification (WELL Platinum, IGBC Green Interior Platinum & RESET AIR) while embedding wellbeing into every layer of spatial and organisational design.

Client

M Moser Associates, Bengaluru

Scope

649 m² · Two-floor Living Lab

Year

2023 · Ongoing iteration

Scroll

The brief

Three systemic tensions to resolve

01

Behaviour

How do you design a space that genuinely supports diverse work modes (deep focus, casual collision, client-facing formality) without defaulting to assigned desks and status-signalling?

02

Environment

Bengaluru's urban heat island, air quality pressures and high occupant density required active engineering strategies that could be measured, not just claimed on a specification sheet.

03

Culture

The space needed to honour local identity: Bengaluru's craft heritage, biophilic richness, festival rhythms, while demonstrating globally credible workplace innovation.

How we worked

A living lab process iterative by design

01

Employee research & occupancy analysis

Internal surveys and environmental studies revealed that daylight ranked as the single highest aspiration. Occupancy heat-mapping informed free addressing ratios and zone proportions before a single workstation was specified.

Bricks · Data
02

Zoning strategy — activity, not hierarchy

The lower floor was designed for teamwork, client engagement and informal collision. The upper floor reserved for focus, restoration and flexible solo work. Zoning was behaviourally validated, not architecturally assumed.

Behaviour · ABW
03

Daylight modelling & spatial simulation

A 1.5 to 2m sunlight buffer zone was introduced based on glare and thermal modelling. High reflectance materials and transparent partitions carried daylight 30% deeper than the base build allowed.

Bricks · Engineering
04

HR policy as a WELL certification lever

Flexible working hours, ergonomics orientation sessions, nutrition education programmes, and mental health promotion were embedded into HWSS policy, making people practices a measurable part of certification evidence.

Behaviour · WELL
05

Active MEP & real-time air quality monitoring

HVAC targets 30–60% RH with responsive BMS. PM2.5, PM10, CO₂, VOC and temperature data streams live via QLEAR throughout the office — making environmental performance visible and accountable to occupants.

Bytes · RESET AIR
06

Feedback loops for continuous iteration

Post-occupancy surveys, acoustic testing, and real-time sensor data feed an ongoing adaptation cycle. The Bengaluru office functions as a living instrument, evidence for internal innovation and client credibility.

Living Lab methodology

WELL categories addressed

Wellbeing as a systems problem

The IGBC & WELL documentation I led spans six wellbeing domains, translating spatial design decisions into auditable evidence across both the physical environment and HR policy.

🧠

Emotional & intellectual wellbeing

Flexible WFH policy, ergonomics onboarding sessions, mental health promotion initiatives and a Wellness Library, HR policy as spatial strategy.

🌿

Social & cultural wellbeing

Festival celebration spaces, recreation programming and handcrafted local materials creating cultural identity and belonging across the team.

🏃

Physical & thermal comfort

Balance boards, anti fatigue mats, cork yoga mats and foam rollers provided alongside ergonomic workstations and standing desks throughout.

💧

Nourishment & hydration

Nutrition education via WELL N07, seasonal food literacy sessions, a wellness library of nutrition resources, and elimination of single use bottled water.

🌬️

Air quality (RESET certified)

Live PM2.5, PM10, CO₂, VOC and humidity dashboards at 1 µg/m³. BMS integrated responsive HVAC with third-party QLEAR monitoring.

☀️

Daylight & biophilia

Sunlight buffer zones, three sided exposure, mature tree canopy views and high reflectance materials, daylight as the top ranked employee aspiration, delivered.

Why this matters to you

Reading this project through the ABW lens

Bricks · Bytes · Behaviour

Bricks

Spatial strategy as behavioural infrastructure

The two floor zoning model was not an aesthetic decision. It was a behavioural one, separating collaborative and restorative modes after occupancy research proved that people couldn't self regulate in undifferentiated open space. This is Activity Based Working logic applied to floorplate strategy.

Bytes

Continuous data as a management practice

Real time QLEAR dashboards, post occupancy surveys and RESET monitoring created a feedback loop that makes the built environment responsive rather than static. Bytes don't just support the workspace, they are part of how it functions.

Behaviour

HR policy aligned to spatial strategy

Flexible working hours, onboarding ergonomics sessions, nutrition literacy programming and mental health initiatives were not perks, they were WELL certification evidence. Behaviour change was embedded into policy, not hoped for from a floor plan.
